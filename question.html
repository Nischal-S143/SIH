Google Interview Guide

Google's Interview Process

Google's interview process typically consists of the following stages:

  1. Recruiter Screening: A brief call with a recruiter to discuss your background and interest in Google.
  2. Technical Phone Screen: One or two 45-minute technical interviews focusing on algorithms and data structures.
  3. Onsite Interviews: 4-5 interviews covering coding, system design, and behavioral questions.
  4. Hiring Committee Review: Your packet is reviewed by a committee who makes the final decision.

What Google Looks For

  • Technical Proficiency: Strong coding skills in one programming language
  • Problem-Solving Ability: Approach problems systematically and optimize solutions
  • Culture Fit: Alignment with Google's values and collaborative spirit
  • Communication Skills: Ability to explain your thought process clearly
